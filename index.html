What is an Immigrant Detention Center?

Immigrant Detention Centers are where immigrants are kept if they are suspected of visa violations, unauthorized entry, and individuals waiting on a decision made regarding their deportation.

What is Happening and Why Are We Concerned

Reported by Madeline Joune from The Times, adults and children have been cramped in cages, where they may be held for days, weeks, and even month long periods. In the cages they aren't given bare necessities like soap, toothpaste, or access to water. The Department of Homeland Security’s inspectors found 900 people in a space that can only hold 125. Babies drink from unsanitized bottles with almost no diapers. Lights are kept on for 24 hours a day and temperatures remain extremely cold. Along with these inhuman conditions, the flu, lice, chicken pox and scabies have been an ongoing problem. Because the sick aren't separated from those with good healthy and medical care is hard to comeby, these diseases pose even more of a threat. Due to new rules, people can only be kept for a maximum of 72 hours, however this is often surpassed. To make up for it lack of space, tent cities were created and unused buildings (not meant for people to be living in) were converted into storage spaces for immigrants.


Mariee's Story

In 2018 Mariee Juárez, a 2 year old in the detention camp, died of what her mother, Yazmin Juárez considered to be a perventable death. Initially, the mother-daughter duo fled from Guadelope, in hopes for a better looking future. They were kept in a cage with 30 other people. Mariee was told to be in good health when first checked by the doctors. They were then admitted to Family Residential Center in Dilley where Yazmin noted, "no effort was being made to separate the sick from the healthy or to care for them." After a week of their stay, Mariee developed a cough. As a concerned mother Yazmin brought Mariee to a clinic, where they perscribed honey and Tylenol for her resporitory infection. Mariee worstened, and ten days later her mother said she'd lost 8% of her body fat. After returning to the clinic and Mariee being rediagnosed with an ear infection, Yazmin begging the doctors to do further exams, but the medical staff declined. Mariee ended up on the ventilator for 6 weeks because of a resporatory infection, when she later died on Mother's Day.
