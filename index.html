What is a Immigrant Detention Center?

Immigrant Detention Centers are where Immigrants are kept if they are suspected of visa violations, unauthorized entry, and individuals waiting on a decision made regarding their deportation.

What is Happening and Why Are We Concerned

Reported by Madeline Joune from the times, adults and children have been cramped in cages, where they may be held for days, weeks, and even month long periods. In the cages they aren't even bare necessities like soap, toothpaste, or access to water. Department of Homeland Securityâ€™s inspector found 900 people in a space that can only hold 125. Babies drink from unsanitized bottles with almost no diapers. Lights are kept on for 24 hours a day and temperatures remain extremely cold. Along with these inhuman conditions, the flu, lice, chicken pox and scabies have been an ongoing problem. Because the sick aren't separated from those with good healthy and medical care isn't amazing, these diseases pose even more of a threat. Due to new rules, people can only be kept for a maximum of 72 hours, however this time period is often surpassed. To make up for it tent cities were created, and unused buildings (not meant for people to be living in) were converted into storage spaces for immigrants.
